  • Understood how hobbies and interests are connected to professional competencies and soft skills
  • Practiced introspection by answering questions on failure, success, relationships, and purpose
🎤 Unit 2: Dos, What & Whys of Presentation
  • Learned the core DOs of a presentation: know your audience, structure content logically, and rehearse beforehand
  • Understood the DON'Ts: reading off slides, overloading content, using cluttered visuals, and speaking too fast
  • Understood WHAT a presentation is — a purposeful, audience-centered transfer of information or ideas
  • Learned WHY presentations matter: they build credibility, influence decisions, and demonstrate expertise
  • Understood the importance of the opening hook — capturing attention in the first 30 seconds
  • Learned how to design clean slides: one idea per slide, minimal text, clear visuals, and consistent theme
  • Practiced using body language — posture, gestures, eye contact — to reinforce spoken content
  • Understood how vocal variety (pace, tone, pauses) keeps an audience engaged throughout
  • Learned the Q&A etiquette — how to handle tough or unexpected questions confidently and gracefully
  • Understood the importance of closing strong — summarizing key points and leaving a memorable final impression
⚡ Unit 3: Extempore & PESTEL Analysis
  • Understood WHAT extempore speaking is — an impromptu talk delivered with little or no preparation time
  • Learned WHEN extempore speaking is used: job interviews, GDs, boardroom updates, and campus competitions
  • Understood WHO benefits — professionals who need to think on their feet and communicate under pressure
  • Learned WHOSE feedback matters — audience cues, body language, and reactions guide live adjustments
  • Practiced structuring an extempore response using the PREP method: Point, Reason, Example, Point
  • Understood PESTEL as a strategic macro-environment analysis framework: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal
  • Learned how Political factors (government policies, trade regulations, political stability) affect business decisions
  • Understood how Economic indicators (GDP, inflation, interest rates, employment) shape industry opportunities and risks
  • Analyzed how Social trends (demographics, culture, lifestyle changes) influence consumer behavior and market demand
  • Explored Technological, Environmental, and Legal dimensions of PESTEL — how innovation, climate concerns, and laws create or limit business opportunities
🗣️ Unit 4: Group Discussion
  • Understood the purpose of a GD: it evaluates communication, leadership, critical thinking, and teamwork simultaneously
  • Learned the key roles in a GD — initiator, summarizer, devil's advocate, moderator — and when to play each
  • Understood how to initiate a group discussion with a strong, relevant, data-backed opening statement
  • Learned techniques to enter an ongoing discussion without interrupting rudely — pausing, bridging, and using entry phrases
  • Practiced how to build on others' points to show collaborative thinking rather than just asserting your own view
  • Understood how to handle aggressive or dominating participants calmly and assertively
  • Learned how to stay relevant — steering the discussion back on track when it goes off-topic
  • Understood the difference between a fishy argument and a well-substantiated point backed by facts
  • Learned the art of summarizing a GD effectively — capturing all key viewpoints without bias
  • Understood common GD mistakes: speaking too long, going off-topic, nodding without contributing, and personalizing disagreements
✉️ Unit 5: 7Cs of Communication & Cover Letter
  • Learned the 7Cs of Communication: Clear, Concise, Concrete, Correct, Coherent, Complete, and Courteous
  • Understood Clarity — using simple language and avoiding jargon to ensure the message is easily understood by the receiver
  • Practiced Conciseness — saying more with fewer words by eliminating redundancy and filler phrases
  • Applied Concreteness — using specific facts, data, and examples instead of vague or abstract statements
  • Understood Correctness — ensuring grammar, facts, and tone are accurate to maintain credibility and professionalism
  • Learned Coherence — structuring messages logically so ideas flow naturally from one point to the next
  • Practiced Completeness — ensuring all necessary information is included so the receiver has no unanswered questions
  • Understood Courtesy — being respectful, empathetic, and considerate in tone, especially in written professional communication
  • Learned the structure of a cover letter: opening hook, skills-to-role alignment, value proposition, and a confident call to action
  • Understood how a cover letter differs from a resume — it tells your story, shows personality, and explains WHY you want the specific role
